Our recommendations for your stay in Rome

We are so happy that you chose to come to Rome and celebrate this important day with us! We would like to help you to make the most of your trip by recommending a few of our favourite places to you, and providing some practical information to your trip.

Culinary experience

Aperitivo is a popular italian tradition. In most places you can simply order some drinks and access the free buffet of food. An example of classic roman dishes: Pasta Carbonara, Pasta Cacio & Pepe, Pasta Amatriciana, Code alla vaccinara (Ox tail in tomato sauce), Carciofi alla Giudea or Romana (Artichokes deep-fried or steamed). Fried Zucchini Flowers (Fiori Di Zucca). Olive Ascolane (fried olives with meat inside).

Il Sorpasso is a recommended place for an aperitivo

If you are close to Sant Peter and you get hungry, this is a great place to get a classic Roman style slice of pizza to go! Pizza Ricca

Il Duca in Trastevere is definitely a great restaurant offering authentic Roman food

Our favourite coffee shops

In Rome you can get coffee in almost any bar during the day, and it's generally very good. You can't get coffee to go from these kind of bars, but you can consume it standing at the bar or sitting. Cappuccino are generally only consumed before 11AM otherwise the barista wil yell at you! You can always order espresso, and if you want milk you orde espresso macchiato. If you want a large coffee and not an espresso, ask for an "Americano"

Caffè Greco - One of the oldest coffee shop in Europe. The prices are above the average but inside is basically a Museum

A more moder style coffee shop is Pergamino Caffe

Our favourite spots

Gastroni - One of the oldest specialty grocery stores in Rome

The old Getto in Rome is a beautiful place to try some authentic roman food. This restaurant is Kosher and highly recommended - Casalino Osteria Kosher

Trastevere is definitely one of our favourite places in Rome. It's a very old part of Rome, with a lot of restaurants and pubs. It's where romans hang out at night.

Definitely take a walk around Castel Sant'Angelo and along the Tiber river

If it's a good day to be outdoors, definitely spend some time to the Palatino hill. You can buy a combined ticket to access the Colosseum, skipping most of the queue. Legendary founding site of the city & home to ruins of Roman Imperial palaces, temples & baths.

Practical information

In case of an emergency, dial 112. You can speak English

This is a message for teh Bostonians: do not jaywalk in Italy, or you will get hurt. Use cross walks.

If you take public transportation, please keep a close eye on your valuables, especially when buying tickets at the machines at the stations.

If you are staying in Prati neighbour (city center), Rome is very walkable, and you don't need to take taxis to reach most places of interest

Uber is available in Rome and it's cheaper than official Taxis
